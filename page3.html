Sir Robert Bryson Hall II (Logic) Logic

Information

Sir Robert Bryson Hall II, also known as Logic, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer. Logic has been making music since 2009. He married Jessica Andrea in 2015, but sadly they recently divorced. He currently resides in Beverly Hills, California.

Early Life

Logic was born on January 22, 1990 at Shady Grove Hospital in Rockville, Maryland. He spent a lot of his childhood in the West Deer Park neighborhood in Gaithersburg, Maryland. During the early years of his life, Logic witnessed his brothers produce and distribute crack cocaine to "addicts all over the block," as well as witnessing them sell these drugs to their father.Eventually, Logic was expelled from Gathersburg High School in his Sophemore year for skipping classes. He stated, "I started doing badly and failed every class but English, so they kicked me out of school, they gave up on me."

Career

Logic became interested in rap and hip hop after watching the movie Kill Bill: Volume 1.In 2009, Logic performed under the stage name "Psychological" with Logic describing the name as "one name that really stuck [with me]. He then released an unofficial mixtape titled Psychological-Logic: The Mixtape. On December 15, 2010, Logic finally released his first official mixtape, Young, Broke & Infamous, which allowed him to sign with Visionary Music Group. Logic released three more mixtapes with this label before signing with Def Jam Recordings for his work on Young Sinatra: Welcome to Forever (2013). Logic has released multiple mixtapes and albums, Under Pressure (2014), The Incredible True Story (2015), Bobby Tarantino (2016), Everybody (2017), and Bobby Tarantino II (2018), with this label. Logic has been nominated for 5 awards, but has only won twice.
